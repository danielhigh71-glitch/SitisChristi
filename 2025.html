2025 Updates

Mobility, clean water, and a first step into prosthetics.

In 2025, Sitis Christi supported three practical projects focused on mobility and access to clean water. A prosthetic leg and two rural water taps improved daily life for more than 1,800 people.

Waterloo, Sierra Leone
Story 01

Water Tap for Apostolic Christian School

At Apostolic Christian School, more than 1,000 students did not have on-site access to drinking water. Students carried water from home or left school grounds to find it, which interrupted learning, safety, and hygiene.

Sitis Christi funded a dedicated school water tap for drinking water and restroom hygiene. The tap supports a safer and more consistent school day for students and staff.

Clean water for ~1,000 students daily
Improved restroom hygiene and sanitation
Reduced need for students to leave school grounds
More consistent learning environment

Waterloo, Sierra Leone
Story 02

Rural Water Tap, Waterloo

In a rural community in Waterloo, residents had limited access to clean water and often relied on distant or unsafe sources. A new water tap helped address that need.

Sitis Christi funded the installation of a community tap that now provides clean, reliable water for up to 800 villagers every day. The tap reduces time spent collecting water and supports safer daily routines for families.

Clean water for ~800 villagers daily
Reduced waterborne illness risk
Closer access for women and children
Time freed for work, school, and family
Mexico
Story 03

A Prosthetic Leg for Margarito

First amputee supported by Sitis Christi

Margarito, an amputee in Mexico, had been unable to return to his work as a bus driver. Without a prosthetic leg, he faced limits on mobility and income.

Sitis Christi provided a prosthetic leg, fitted and adjusted for his daily needs. With it, Margarito was able to return to work and move through daily life with greater independence.

Margarito was the first amputee supported through this work. His prosthetic was important because it introduced Sitis Christi to prosthetics — an area the organization became heavily involved with in 2026.

One prosthetic leg provided and fitted
Full mobility restored
Employment regained as a bus driver
Greater day-to-day stability
Identified additional amputees with similar needs

"One prosthetic leg and two water taps. Together, they improved mobility, access to water, hygiene, and daily routines for children, families, and residents in the communities served."
