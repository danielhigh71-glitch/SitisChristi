2026 Updates

Overview

2026 is still in progress, but the year has already marked a shift in our work with amputees and children who struggle with mobility. The interventions so far have been practical and focused on helping people move, work, and manage daily life with more stability.

Waterloo, Sierra Leone
Story 01

Food Support for the Waterloo Amputee Shelter

Sitis Christi has become the primary sponsor of food for the Waterloo amputee shelter. The shelter houses ten amputee men. This support provides consistent meals and reduces the daily uncertainty they have lived with for years.

Reliable food support for ten amputee men — ongoing
More stable daily living conditions
Sierra Leone
Story 02

David: Prosthetics, Wheelchair, and Physical Therapy

David is 13 and lost both legs when he was 5. For years he walked to school on his stubs. This year, funding provided prosthetic legs and a wheelchair. His legs have been fitted, and he is now in therapy and gait training before returning home.

"For David, this is the first time in years that walking will not mean pain or exhaustion."
Prosthetic legs built and fitted
Wheelchair delivered
Therapy and gait training in progress
Return to school and daily life made possible
Sierra Leone
Story 03

Joseph: A Wheelchair for a Child With a Rare Bone Condition

Joseph is a 12-year-old student born with a rare growth plate disorder that affects how the ends of his leg bones form. His knee joints remain unusually small and irregular. His legs are weak and painful, and he can walk only short distances.

A wheelchair will soon be provided to help him move safely, attend school, and get through his day without the strain he currently faces.

Wheelchair funded
Safe mobility for school and home
Reduced daily pain

"2026 is becoming the year we stepped deeper into the world of amputee support. This happened not by planning but by following the needs in front of us. Each case, whether a prosthetic limb, a wheelchair, or a month of food, is a small hinge that shifts the direction of a life."

2026 Reflection — Sitis Christi
